How We Make Bricks

01

Clay Preparation

The raw red clay is collected into a massive pit and mixed with water. A tractor repeatedly moves over the clay, compressing and mixing it to achieve the proper consistency for high-strength moulding.

02

Brick Moulding

Workers manually shape the prepared clay into wood moulds. Solid compaction by experienced hands ensures sharp corners, uniform rectangular shape, and accurate sizing.

03

Natural Drying

Freshly moulded green bricks are arranged in neat, long rows in our spacious outdoor drying yard. Natural sun and air drying gradually reduces moisture to prevent structural cracking during firing.

04

Kiln Firing

The bricks are cured inside a traditional brick kiln. Firewood fuel is inserted manually through multiple lower furnace openings, burning at peak heat to fuse raw minerals into solid ceramic blocks.

05

Finished Red Bricks

After cooling, fully cured red clay bricks are sorted. The resulting products exhibit a rich terracotta coloration, high structural density, and are ready for load-bearing applications.

06

Loading & Delivery

Workers manually stack and load finished bricks onto our flatbed delivery vehicles. Hand-loading prevents brick breakage, ensuring your batch arrives at your site in perfect condition.
